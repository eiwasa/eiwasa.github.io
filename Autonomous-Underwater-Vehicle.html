Autonomous underwater Vehicle Project
January 2022 - May 2022

Overview

For Harvey Mudd's Class, E80: Experimental Engineering, we designed and deployed Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUV). My team developed an AUV that measured temperature and turbidity with respect to depth.

As our team's electronics lead, I designed all of our circuits. This process required inintegrating sensor components of interest, such as thermistors, pressure sensors, photodiodes, and timers. For our AUV, we read data using a Teensy 3.2. This meant that the output voltages for each sensor had to be within a certain range (0V to 3.3), while also providing a large enough output swing to show clear variations in data. The designs were validated using simulations and experimental trials.
